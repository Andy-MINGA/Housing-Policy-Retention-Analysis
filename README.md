## Overview
This repository contains a comprehensive analysis of housing residency policies and their correlation with student retention across 22 U.S. universities (2023–2025). The research benchmarks institutional policies to identify which approaches, ranging from voluntary housing to two-year mandates most effectively support freshman-to-sophomore persistence.

---

## Research Objective
To determine whether sophomore residency mandates serve as an effective administrative lever for improving student retention, providing actionable insights for university leadership and housing policy design.

---

## Key Findings
- **U-Shaped Retention Pattern:** Both fully voluntary and two-year housing policies outperform the one-year freshman-only mandate.  
- **Retention Lift:** Tier 3 (two-year mandate) institutions show a +1.4% increase in freshman-to-sophomore retention compared to one-year mandates.  
- **Choice Matters:** Voluntary participation (Tier 1) can achieve high retention when campus culture and residential experience are strong.

## Dataset Description
This dataset compiles housing residency requirements and first-year retention rates for 22 U.S. universities (2023–2025). The data were used to analyze the relationship between housing mandates (0-year, 1-year, and 2-year requirements) and student retention outcomes. Housing policy information was collected from official university websites, while retention statistics were obtained from institutional reports and the [National Center for Education Statistics](https://nces.ed.gov/).
